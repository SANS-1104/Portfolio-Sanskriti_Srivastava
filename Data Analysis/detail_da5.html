Project Description

I conducted an in-depth Air Quality Analysis by processing and visualizing air pollution data using Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) and Power BI. The dataset included AQI values across multiple years and locations. By cleaning, transforming, and analyzing key pollutants like PM2.5, CO, and NO2, I uncovered critical air quality trends. The Power BI dashboard provided interactive insights, making it easier to identify pollution hotspots and seasonal variations. This project demonstrates my ability to handle large datasets, apply data visualization techniques, and generate actionable insights for environmental and policy-related decision-making.

Key Insights

1. Quantitative Metrics

  - Average AQI across all locations: 120.45

  - Most polluted city (2019): Delhi - AQI 398

2. AQI Trend by Year

  - AQI levels peaked in winter months, indicating higher pollution during colder seasons.

  - mprovement in AQI observed during COVID-19 lockdown (2020) due to reduced human activity.

3. Major Pollutants Impacting AQI

  - PM2.5 and CO were the strongest contributors to poor air quality.

  - Cities with higher industrialization had elevated levels of SO2 and NO2.

4. AQI by Region

  - Ahmedabad recorded an extreme AQI spike (>2000) in certain months.

  - Coastal cities had better air quality due to natural dispersion of pollutants.

5. Policy Implications & Recommendations

  - Stricter vehicular emission norms needed for metro cities.

  - Encouraging green energy adoption could significantly lower AQI levels.
